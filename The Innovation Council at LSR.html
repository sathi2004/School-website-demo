The Innovation Council at LSR

LSR’s Innovation Council:

LSR’s Innovation Council “Innovation is the creation of something that improves the way we live our lives”

LSR was selected by the MHRD in October 2018 for the constitution of an Innovation Council to promote innovation among its students. This year, the faculty attended online sessions conducted by the Innovation Council of the MoE’s Innovation Cell, to guide and mentor students on IPR, innovation, entrepreneurship and start-ups under the “IIC-Innovation Ambassador Program 2021.” These sessions which took place from 30th June 2021 to 30th July 2021 were as follows:

  1. I and E Initiatives and Programs of MoE for HEIs
  2. National Innovation and Startup Policy (NISP): Guiding Framework for HEIs
  3. National Education Policy (NEP): Research, Innovation and Entrepreneurship for HEIs
  4. Integration of Research, Innovation and Entrepreneurship in Academic Institutions and Economic Growth of Country
  5. Attaining Problem-Solution Fit and Market-Product Fit: Prototype Designing Process and Tools for Development of Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
  6. Understanding Technology Readiness Level (TRL), Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL) and Investment Readiness Level (IRL): Stages and Implications in Innovation Development
  7. Strengthening the connection between School and Higher Education Institutes: Role of Institutions & Teachers
  8. Innovation Management Process and Approaches at early Stages of Development of Innovation and Startup
  9. Business Plan Development: Components and Stages in Business Plan Development
  10. Validation of Business Opportunity: Application of Marketing Research Tools at the Early Stage of Startup – Case Study
  11. Attaining Business Fit: Startup Planning and Management
  12. Generation and Management of IP at the Early Stage of Innovation and Startup Development
  13. Legal Structures, Financial Risk Management, and Ethical Steps in Establishing Startups
  14. Planning and Managing Incubation Support System in HEI & Resource Mobilization Strategy
  15. Planning and Managing IPR Facility in HEIs: Technology Transfer Oﬃce (TTO) Model
  16. Roles and Responsibility of Faculty Innovation Ambassadors: Faculty as Mentor and Engagement in Post Training Activities


Faculty and students successfully attended the “National Webinar on Research, Innovation and Ranking” with Dr. Rajkumar Ranjan Singh, Minister of State for Education and External Aﬀairs, and Prof. K. Vijay Raghavan, Principal Scientific Advisor, Govt. of India hosted by MIC on 11th August 2021. They also successfully attended the Innovation Week Celebration ‘Azaadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav.’ There was a 2-day symposium ‘Building Innovation Ecosystem in Educational Institutions” on 11th and 12th January 2022 by the Ministry of Education. The program was held virtually and saw huge participation from academic institutions, schools, industry, start-ups, and the investor community. Further IIC-LSR conducted an interactive online session on “Innovation: Trending Up But Needs Thrust” by Ms. Deepika Srivastava, Officer of the Indian Economic Service, Government of India and currently posted as Deputy Director in Department of Economic Aﬀairs, Ministry of Finance on 15th February, 2022.
